#  It Doesn't Have To Be Fancy
Welcome to **day 68** of 365 days of code - coding every day for a year, little and often

I definitely need to remind myself sometimes that it's ***little*** and often, not lots and often. After a full on day, I was sort of regretting that I still had to do today's coding, then an idea occurred to me while I was doing dishes, a simple little thing, that actually avoids a big problem down the track. And it's like 6 lines of code.

I need to remember that I don't have to push myself so hard all the time, it's a busy life, and there's lots on, this is supposed to be something I'm doing for me, but it can't take over. I don't need to launch a big feature every day. I don't need to do a PR every day. I have a busy family life. Small, incremental change every day is the goal.

Anyway, today I moved all my default settings values into a single constant.

That's it... I wrote the constant (in it's own defaults file), referenced it in the two places (so far) that use them. And...done.

It will save future me when I decide one day to change a default in one place, and then either have to go find everywhere else that it exists, or forget to and cause at best a weird experience or at worst a crash.
